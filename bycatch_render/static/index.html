The Problem

A crisis hidden beneath the surface

Sea Turtles

All seven species of sea turtle are threatened or endangered. Longline and trawl fisheries in NC waters are a leading cause of injury and mortality.

Indiscriminate Gear

Bottom trawls and gillnets can't distinguish between target and non-target species. Everything in their path is pulled aboard — most of it thrown back dead.

Marine Mammals

Dolphins, porpoises, and whales become entangled in fishing gear every day. Many drown before they can be freed. North Carolina's coast is a hotspot.

North Carolina

Our coast. Our responsibility.

The NC coast stretches 300+ miles, home to some of the Atlantic's richest fishing grounds — and its most vulnerable ecosystems. The Outer Banks, Pamlico Sound, and Cape Hatteras lie in the migration corridors of loggerhead turtles, spotted dolphins, and humpback whales.

Commercial fishing here generates over $100M per year, but the hidden cost in bycatch is staggering. Data from the NC Division of Marine Fisheries shows tens of thousands of non-target interactions annually.
