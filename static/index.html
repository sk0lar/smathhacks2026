The Problem

A crisis hidden beneath the surface

🐢

Sea Turtles

All seven species of sea turtle are threatened or endangered. Longline and trawl fisheries off the NC coast are a leading cause of injury and mortality.

🎣

Indiscriminate Gear

Bottom trawls and gillnets cannot distinguish target from non-target species. Everything in their path is hauled aboard — most of it thrown back dead.

🐬

Marine Mammals

Dolphins, porpoises, and whales entangle in fishing gear daily. Many drown before they can be freed. North Carolina's coast is a major hotspot.

North Carolina

Our coast.
Our responsibility.

The NC coastline stretches 300+ miles, home to some of the Atlantic's richest fishing grounds — and its most fragile ecosystems. The Outer Banks, Pamlico Sound, and Cape Hatteras sit in the migration corridors of loggerhead turtles, spotted dolphins, and humpback whales.

Commercial fishing here generates over $100M per year — but the hidden cost in bycatch is enormous. NC DMF data shows tens of thousands of non-target interactions every year.
